# 📘 Chapter 1 — Legal Procedure

## Chapter Prerequisites

Terms a student must already understand before entering Diagram 1. Diagram-specific terms are released inside that diagram.

| # | Term | Plain meaning |
|---|------|----------------|
| 1 | **Inquest** | A legal enquiry held to find out the cause and circumstances of a sudden, suspicious or unnatural death. |
| 2 | **Court** | A government-established body that hears legal disputes and decides them according to law. |
| 3 | **Magistrate** | A judicial officer (of various ranks) who tries cases, issues warrants, or conducts legal enquiries. |
| 4 | **Summons** | A formal written order from a court directing a person to appear before it on a specified date. |
| 5 | **Subpoena** | The foreign-jurisdiction equivalent of a summons — a written legal document compelling attendance in court or production of documents. |
| 6 | **Cognizable offense** | A serious crime (e.g. murder, rape) in which police can arrest the accused without a warrant. |
| 7 | **Witness** | Any person who appears in court and gives evidence about facts within his knowledge. |
| 8 | **Oath** | A solemn declaration to tell the truth, sworn before giving evidence in court. |
| 9 | **Evidence** | Anything (oral, documentary, or material) presented in court to prove or disprove a fact. |
| 10 | **Affidavit** | A written statement made under oath, used in court as a form of documentary evidence. |
