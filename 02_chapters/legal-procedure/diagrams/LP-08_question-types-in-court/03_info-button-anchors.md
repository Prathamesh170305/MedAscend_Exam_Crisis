# LP-08 — ⓘ-Button Anchors

---

### NODE: Leading Question
- **Start sentence:** "A leading question is one which suggests the answer that the questioner wishes to receive."
- **End sentence:** "It is allowed in cross-examination but generally not permitted in examination-in-chief or re-examination."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Leading Question; Source B p.10 -->

### NODE: Hypothetical Question
- **Start sentence:** "A hypothetical question is one which is based on certain assumed facts that have been proved or are likely to be proved."
- **End sentence:** "It is mainly used to elicit the opinion of an expert witness on the assumed facts."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Hypothetical Question; Source B p.10 -->

### NODE: Compound Question
- **Start sentence:** "A compound question is one which contains two or more questions clubbed into a single one."
- **End sentence:** "Such a question is liable to be disallowed by the judge as it tends to confuse the witness."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Compound Question; Source B p.10 -->

### NODE: Indecent / Insulting Question
- **Start sentence:** "Indecent or scandalous questions, which have no bearing on the facts in issue, shall be forbidden by the court."
- **End sentence:** "However, indecent or scandalous questions relating to the facts in issue or to matters necessary to be known to determine those facts may be allowed."
