# LP-08 — MCQs (Question Types)

---

**Q1.** "Doctor, if a stab wound of 5 cm depth is found in the chest, would it be fatal?" — This is an example of:
A) Leading question
B) Hypothetical question
C) Compound question
D) Indecent question

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Hypothetical question**
💡 Based on assumed facts, used to elicit an expert's opinion — the defining feature of a hypothetical question.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q2.** A question that bundles two or more queries into one, liable to be disallowed by the court, is:
A) Compound question
B) Leading question
C) Recall question
D) Insulting question

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: A — Compound question**
💡 The court may disallow a compound question because it confuses the witness and produces an ambiguous answer.
🏷️ Tag: `[UNIV]`

---

**Q3.** Leading questions are **NOT** allowed in:
A) Cross-examination
B) Examination-in-chief
C) Court questions
D) Recall by court

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Examination-in-chief**
💡 Leading Q: not allowed in examination-in-chief and re-examination. Allowed in cross-examination and from the judge.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q4.** An indecent or scandalous question put to a witness:
A) Must always be allowed in cross-examination
B) Must be forbidden by the judge if it has no bearing on the facts in issue
C) Is automatically a leading question
D) Can be put only by an expert witness

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Must be forbidden by the judge if it has no bearing on the facts in issue**
💡 The court has discretion: it MUST forbid indecent questions unrelated to the facts in issue; it MAY allow them if relevant (e.g., in a sexual-offence trial where intimate facts ARE the issue).
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q5.** Which statement about leading questions is **correct**?
A) Allowed only in examination-in-chief
B) Allowed in cross-examination and from the court at any stage
C) Banned entirely in Indian courts
D) Allowed only when the witness is hostile

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Allowed in cross-examination and from the court at any stage**
💡 The pattern: leading Qs are allowed when the **opposing party** (cross-exam) or the **judge** (court questions) asks. Not when the calling party asks (exam-in-chief, re-exam).
🏷️ Tag: `[UNIV]`
