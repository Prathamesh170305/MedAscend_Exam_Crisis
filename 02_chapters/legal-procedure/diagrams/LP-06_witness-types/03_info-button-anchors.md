# LP-06 — ⓘ-Button Anchors

---

### NODE: COMMON WITNESS
- **Start sentence:** "A common witness is one who deposes about facts seen, heard, or otherwise perceived by him."
- **End sentence:** "He is not allowed to express any opinion — he is only an observer."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Common Witness; Source B p.9 -->

### NODE: EXPERT WITNESS
- **Start sentence:** "An expert witness is a person specially skilled in a particular branch of knowledge, who is permitted by law to give his opinion in court."
- **End sentence:** "Under section 45 of the Indian Evidence Act (now section 39 of the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam, 2023), opinions of persons skilled in science, art, foreign law, handwriting, or finger impressions are relevant facts."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Expert Witness; Source B p.9; current law: BSA 2023 §39 (old IEA §45) -->

### NODE: HOSTILE WITNESS
- **Start sentence:** "A hostile witness is one who, after being called, gives evidence against the case of the party that called him."
- **End sentence:** "With the permission of the court, the party calling him may then cross-examine him as if he were the opposing party's witness."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Hostile Witness; Source B p.9 -->

### NODE: Skilled witness (synonym tag)
- **Start sentence:** "The terms 'expert witness' and 'skilled witness' are used interchangeably in Indian medicolegal practice."
- **End sentence:** "A doctor giving evidence about injuries, cause of death, or fitness in court is appearing as an expert / skilled witness."
