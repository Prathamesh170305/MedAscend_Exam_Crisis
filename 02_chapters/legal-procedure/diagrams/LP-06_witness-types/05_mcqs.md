# LP-06 — MCQs (Witness Types)

---

**Q1.** A doctor giving evidence in court about the injuries he examined is appearing as a:
A) Common witness
B) Expert (skilled) witness
C) Hostile witness
D) Eyewitness

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Expert (skilled) witness**
💡 A doctor in court = expert witness. The law (BSA §39, old IEA §45) permits him to give opinion. A common witness deposes only about facts perceived; he cannot opine.

---

**Q2.** Expert evidence is admissible under which section of the 2023 evidence code?
A) BSA §26
B) BSA §39
C) BSA §45
D) IEA §32

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — BSA §39**
💡 Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam 2023 §39 (renumbered from old IEA §45) covers expert opinions. BSA §26 (old IEA §32) covers dying declaration.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q3.** When a witness, with the court's permission, is cross-examined by the very party that called him, he is termed a:
A) Common witness
B) Hostile witness
C) Expert witness
D) Eyewitness

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Hostile witness**
💡 A hostile witness has deposed against the case of the party that called him; only after the judge declares him hostile may his own side cross-examine him.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q4.** A passer-by who saw a road traffic accident and describes what he saw in court is a:
A) Expert witness
B) Common witness (witness of fact)
C) Hostile witness
D) Skilled witness

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Common witness (witness of fact)**
💡 He is describing facts directly perceived — that defines a common witness. He cannot opine on cause or speed unless he is an accident-reconstruction expert.

---

**Q5.** All of the following statements about an expert witness are correct **except**:
A) He can give opinion in his area of expertise
B) He must be specially skilled in the subject
C) He cannot depose about facts he personally perceived
D) Doctor in court is treated as an expert witness

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: C — He cannot depose about facts he personally perceived**
💡 An expert witness can depose about both his observations AND his opinion. Restriction to facts only applies to the common witness.
