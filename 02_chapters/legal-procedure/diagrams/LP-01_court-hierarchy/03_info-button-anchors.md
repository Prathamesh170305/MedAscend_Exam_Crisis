# LP-01 — ⓘ-Button Anchors

Vedant's workflow: Ctrl+F the start sentence in the source → copy block to end sentence → paraphrase pointwise → paste into the ⓘ-button content field. The student sees only the paraphrased content.

---

### NODE: Supreme Court of India
- **Start sentence:** "The Supreme Court is the highest court of appeal in India."
- **End sentence:** "It can also try cases of original jurisdiction in matters between states or between the centre and a state."
<!-- Backend source: Source A (Reddy 35th E) — chapter on Courts of Law; Source B (Bardale Ch 1) p.4 -->

### NODE: High Court
- **Start sentence:** "The High Court is the highest court of a state."
- **End sentence:** "Every sentence of death passed by a Sessions Court must be confirmed by the High Court before it can be executed."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Courts; Source B p.4 -->

### NODE: Sessions Court / Additional Sessions Court
- **Start sentence:** "The Sessions Court is the highest criminal court in a district."
- **End sentence:** "It may pass any sentence authorised by law, but a death sentence requires confirmation by the High Court."
<!-- Backend source: Source A; Source B p.5 (Bardale: "Court of Sessions — passes any sentence including death") -->

### NODE: Assistant Sessions Court
- **Start sentence:** "Assistant Sessions Judge tries cases referred to him by the Sessions Judge."
- **End sentence:** "He may pass any sentence except death, life imprisonment, or imprisonment exceeding 10 years."
<!-- Backend source: Source B p.5 -->

### NODE: Chief Judicial / Chief Metropolitan Magistrate
- **Start sentence:** "The Chief Judicial Magistrate is the head of the Magistrate's courts in a district."
- **End sentence:** "He may pass any sentence authorised by law, except death, life imprisonment, or imprisonment exceeding 7 years."
<!-- Backend source: Source B p.5; cross-check Source A -->

### NODE: Judicial / Metropolitan Magistrate — First Class
- **Start sentence:** "A Judicial Magistrate of First Class may try cases punishable with imprisonment up to three years."
- **End sentence:** "He may also impose a fine not exceeding ten thousand rupees, or both."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Courts of Law; Source B p.5 -->

### NODE: Judicial Magistrate — Second Class
- **Start sentence:** "A Judicial Magistrate of Second Class may pass a sentence of imprisonment for a term not exceeding one year."
- **End sentence:** "He may also impose a fine not exceeding five thousand rupees, or both."
<!-- Backend source: Source A; Source B p.5 -->

### NODE: High Court (Civil side)
- **Start sentence:** "On the civil side, the High Court has both appellate and original jurisdiction."
- **End sentence:** "It hears civil appeals from district courts and from its own single judge in certain cases."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Civil Courts; Source B p.4 -->

### NODE: District Court / Court of Civil Judge
- **Start sentence:** "The District Court is the highest civil court at the district level."
- **End sentence:** "It hears appeals from courts of subordinate civil judges and decides civil suits of higher pecuniary value."
<!-- Backend source: Source A; Source B p.4 -->
