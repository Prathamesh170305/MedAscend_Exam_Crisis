# LP-01 — MCQs (Court Hierarchy)

> No L1 PYQ from the uploaded bank maps to court hierarchy directly. All MCQs below are L2/L3.

---

**Q1.** Death sentence passed by a Sessions Court must be confirmed by which authority before execution?
A) Supreme Court
B) High Court
C) Chief Judicial Magistrate
D) Governor of the State

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — High Court**
💡 Sessions Court may pass any sentence including death, but the death sentence is not executable until the High Court confirms it on appeal.

---

**Q2.** The maximum term of imprisonment a Judicial Magistrate First Class can pass is:
A) 1 year
B) 3 years
C) 7 years
D) 10 years

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — 3 years**
💡 JMFC: up to 3 years imprisonment and/or fine up to ₹10,000. JM-2nd: 1 year / ₹5,000. CJM: up to 7 years. Asst Sessions: up to 10 years.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q3.** Highest criminal court in a district is:
A) High Court
B) Sessions Court
C) Chief Judicial Magistrate Court
D) Judicial Magistrate First Class Court

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Sessions Court**
💡 Sessions Court is the apex criminal court at the district level. The High Court is a state-level court, not district-level.
🏷️ Tag: `[UNIV]`

---

**Q4.** Maximum fine that can be imposed by a Judicial Magistrate Second Class is:
A) ₹1,000
B) ₹5,000
C) ₹10,000
D) ₹50,000

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — ₹5,000**
💡 JM-2nd Class: up to 1 year imprisonment and/or fine up to ₹5,000.
🏷️ Tag: `[UNIV]`

---

**Q5.** A doctor receives a summons from a Metropolitan Magistrate's court for a case of grievous hurt. The accused, if convicted by this magistrate, can be sentenced to a maximum imprisonment of:
A) 1 year
B) 3 years
C) 7 years
D) Any sentence authorised by law

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — 3 years**
💡 A Metropolitan Magistrate has the powers of a Judicial Magistrate First Class — maximum 3 years imprisonment and ₹10,000 fine.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q6.** Highest court of appeal in India is:
A) High Court
B) Supreme Court
C) Sessions Court
D) Constitutional Bench

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Supreme Court**
💡 The Supreme Court of India is the apex court — the final court of appeal. It also has original jurisdiction in disputes between states or between the centre and a state.
🏷️ Tag: `[UNIV]`
