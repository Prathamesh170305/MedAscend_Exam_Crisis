# LP-05 — MCQs (Documentary Evidence Subtypes)

---

**Q1.** Which of the following is **NOT** required while recording a dying declaration?
A) Fit mental state of the declarant
B) Statement to be recorded in declarant's own words
C) Administration of oath
D) Signature / thumb impression of declarant

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: C — Administration of oath**
💡 No oath is administered for a dying declaration — a dying person is presumed to speak the truth. Oath + cross-examination is the defining feature of a dying **deposition**, not declaration.

---

**Q2.** A dying declaration becomes admissible as evidence:
A) Immediately on being recorded
B) Only after the declarant dies
C) Only if recorded by a magistrate
D) Only in cases of poisoning

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Only after the declarant dies**
💡 If the declarant survives, the statement is treated as an ordinary statement; admissibility under BSA §26 (old IEA §32) is contingent on death.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q3.** A dying deposition is recorded:
A) Without oath, by a doctor
B) On oath, in the presence of the accused or his lawyer, by a magistrate
C) Only after the patient has died
D) By police, in the police station

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — On oath, in the presence of the accused or his lawyer, by a magistrate**
💡 Dying deposition = a court-style recording with oath + cross-examination. This is what distinguishes it from a dying declaration and gives it higher evidentiary value.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

**Q4.** A 56-year-old female victim of domestic violence is admitted to hospital with severe injuries. Her statement about the perpetrator is recorded. Under which section of the current evidence code is this dying declaration admissible if she dies?
A) BSA §26
B) BSA §39
C) BSA §45
D) BNSS §174

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: A — BSA §26**
💡 Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam 2023 §26 (renumbered from IEA §32) governs admissibility of a dying declaration. §39 covers expert evidence; BNSS §174 covers police inquest.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

**Q5.** A medicolegal report differs from a medical certificate in that:
A) It is issued by an RMP, certificate is not
B) It is issued only after a police request, certificate is not
C) It is admissible in court, certificate is not
D) It requires Magistrate's signature

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — It is issued only after a police request, certificate is not**
💡 MLR is doctor's written report on a person **referred by police** for a medicolegal purpose. Medical certificate (birth/death/age/fitness) can be issued by any RMP without police referral.

**Q6.** The most important reason oath is not administered to a dying person is:
A) They cannot physically take an oath
B) Indian law forbids oath to the moribund
C) A person about to die is presumed to speak the truth ('nemo moriturus praesumitur mentiri')
D) Magistrate is not authorised to administer oath

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: C — A person about to die is presumed to speak the truth**
💡 The Latin maxim "nemo moriturus praesumitur mentiri" — no one about to die is presumed to lie — is the legal foundation for accepting an un-sworn statement as evidence.
