# LP-05 — ⓘ-Button Anchors

---

### NODE: Medical Certificates
- **Start sentence:** "A medical certificate is a written document issued by a registered medical practitioner regarding birth, death, age, illness, fitness or insanity of a person."
- **End sentence:** "Issuing a false certificate is treated as professional misconduct under the IMC regulations."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Medical Certificates; Source B p.7 -->

### NODE: Medicolegal Report (MLR)
- **Start sentence:** "A medicolegal report is a written report prepared by a doctor about a person referred to him by police for a medicolegal purpose."
- **End sentence:** "Common examples include injury reports, sexual assault examination reports, age estimation, and drunkenness reports."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Medicolegal Reports; Source B p.7 -->

### NODE: Dying Declaration
- **Start sentence:** "A dying declaration is a statement, written or oral, made by a person who is dying, regarding the cause of his death or the circumstances which resulted in his death."
- **End sentence:** "Under BSA section 26 (corresponding to old IEA section 32), such a statement becomes admissible only after the declarant dies, and oath is not required because a dying person is presumed to speak the truth."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Dying Declaration; Source B p.7-8; current law: BSA 2023 §26 (old IEA §32) -->

### NODE: Dying Deposition
- **Start sentence:** "A dying deposition is a statement of a dying person recorded by a magistrate in the form of a regular court deposition."
- **End sentence:** "It is recorded on oath in the presence of the accused or his pleader, who is given the opportunity to cross-examine — giving it a higher evidentiary value than a dying declaration."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Dying Deposition; Source B p.8 -->

### NODE: BSA §26 / IEA §32 (referenced)
- **Start sentence:** "Section 32 of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872 (now corresponding to section 26 of the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam, 2023) permits a dying declaration as an exception to the hearsay rule."
- **End sentence:** "The declarant must have been in a fit mental state to make the statement at the time of recording."
