# LP-03 — ⓘ-Button Anchors

---

### NODE: Summons (document focal point)
- **Start sentence:** "Summons is a written order from a court directing a person to attend the court on a specified date and time."
- **End sentence:** "It is issued in duplicate — the original is served on the person, and the duplicate is signed by him and returned to the court."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Summons section; Source B p.6 -->

### NODE: Subpoena equivalence
- **Start sentence:** "Subpoena is the foreign equivalent of summons."
- **End sentence:** "The word literally means 'under penalty', and disobedience is punishable."
<!-- Backend source: Source A; Source B p.6 -->

### NODE: Witness Summons
- **Start sentence:** "A witness summons compels the recipient to attend the court and depose on oath about facts within his knowledge."
- **End sentence:** "Disobedience without sufficient cause is punishable as contempt of court."
<!-- Backend source: Source A; Source B p.6 -->

### NODE: Production Summons
- **Start sentence:** "A production summons (sometimes called subpoena duces tecum) directs the recipient to produce specified documents or material objects before the court."
- **End sentence:** "Examples include medical records, postmortem reports, radiographs, and medicolegal reports."
<!-- Backend source: Source A; Source B p.6 -->

### NODE: Conduct money
- **Start sentence:** "Conduct money is the sum of money paid to a witness summoned in a civil case to cover his travelling and subsistence expenses."
- **End sentence:** "In criminal cases, no conduct money is paid in advance — expenses are reimbursed by the government after attendance."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Conduct Money; Source B p.6 -->

### NODE: Court seal + signature of judge
- **Start sentence:** "Every summons must bear the seal of the issuing court and the signature of the presiding judicial officer."
- **End sentence:** "Without these, the summons is not legally enforceable."
