# LP-03 — MCQs (Summons / Subpoena)

---

**Q1.** Conduct money is paid to a witness in:
A) Criminal cases only
B) Civil cases only
C) Both civil and criminal cases
D) Only in Sessions Court cases

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Civil cases only**
💡 Conduct money (advance travelling and subsistence allowance) is paid only in **civil** cases. In criminal cases, expenses are reimbursed by the government after the witness has attended court.

**Q2.** A summons that directs a doctor to bring specified medical records to court is called:
A) Witness summons
B) Production summons (subpoena duces tecum)
C) Notice
D) Cognizable warrant

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Production summons (subpoena duces tecum)**
💡 Witness summons → give oral evidence. Production summons (a.k.a. subpoena duces tecum) → produce documents or material objects in court.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q3.** Subpoena is best described as:
A) A police warrant
B) The foreign equivalent of a summons
C) An order to arrest
D) A magistrate's inquest report

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — The foreign equivalent of a summons**
💡 "Subpoena" is the term used in foreign jurisdictions for what Indian law calls a summons. Both compel attendance in court; disobedience is punishable.

---

**Q4.** A summons is **not** legally enforceable without:
A) Court seal and judge's signature
B) Name of opposing party
C) Conduct money attached
D) Police endorsement

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: A — Court seal and judge's signature**
💡 The seal of the issuing court and the signature of the presiding judicial officer are essential — without them the summons is invalid.
🏷️ Tag: `[UNIV]`

---

**Q5.** Disobedience of a duly served summons without sufficient cause is treated as:
A) Civil offence
B) Contempt of court
C) Cognizable offence
D) Medical negligence

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Contempt of court**
💡 Wilful disobedience of a court summons constitutes contempt of court and is punishable accordingly.
