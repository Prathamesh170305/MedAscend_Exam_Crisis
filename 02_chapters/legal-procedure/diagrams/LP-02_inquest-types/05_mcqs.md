# LP-02 — MCQs (Inquest Types)

---

**Q1.** In the event of a death resulting from a road traffic accident, which authority is responsible for requesting an autopsy?
A) Forensic expert
B) Police
C) Lawyer
D) Forensic doctor

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Police**
💡 Under the current police inquest system, the Investigating Officer (Sub-Inspector or above) takes cognizance of any sudden / suspicious / unnatural death — including road traffic accidents — and forwards the body to the government doctor for postmortem.

**Q2.** Which of the following types of inquest is **not** practised in India?
A) Police inquest
B) Magistrate inquest
C) Coroner inquest
D) Both C and Medical Examiner system

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: D — Both C and Medical Examiner system**
💡 Only Police and Magistrate inquests are practised in India. Coroner's inquest was abolished (Mumbai, 29 July 1999); the Medical Examiner system is followed in the USA, not India.

**Q3.** Magistrate inquest is mandatory in all the following situations **except**:
A) Death in police custody
B) Dowry death within 7 years of marriage
C) Death due to road traffic accident
D) Death in a psychiatric hospital

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: C — Death due to road traffic accident**
💡 RTA deaths are handled by police inquest. Magistrate inquest is reserved for custodial deaths, dowry deaths (within 7 yrs), exhumation cases, and deaths in psychiatric hospitals.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

**Q4.** Police inquest is conducted under which section?
A) Section 174 CrPC
B) Section 176 CrPC
C) Section 175 CrPC
D) Section 173 CrPC

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: A — Section 174 CrPC** (now §194 BNSS 2023)
💡 §174 CrPC (renumbered as §194 BNSS) is the legal basis of police inquest. §176 CrPC (now §196 BNSS) covers Magistrate inquest.

**Q5.** The Medical Examiner inquest system is followed in:
A) India
B) United Kingdom
C) United States
D) Australia

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: C — United States**
💡 The Medical Examiner system (conducted by a Forensic Pathologist) is considered the most scientific inquest system and is prevalent in most US states. Not followed in India.

**Q6.** The report prepared by the Investigating Officer at the scene of death in the presence of two responsible local witnesses is called:
A) FIR
B) Panchnama
C) Inquest report
D) Charge-sheet

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Panchnama**
💡 The two or more responsible local persons are called Panchas; the report they witness and sign is the Panchnama. Distinct from the FIR (first information report at police station).
🏷️ Tag: `[UNIV]`

---

**Q7.** An "open verdict" returned by a Coroner means:
A) The Coroner's court is open to public
B) Crime confirmed but accused unknown
C) No crime occurred
D) Body to be re-examined

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Crime confirmed but accused unknown**
💡 When the Coroner finds that a crime has occurred but the accused is not identified, an "open verdict" is returned — announcement of crime without information regarding the accused.
