# LP-09 — MCQs (Conduct of Doctor in Court)

---

**Q1.** Punishment for perjury is under which section of IPC?
A) Sec 191
B) Sec 192
C) Sec 193
D) Sec 197

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: C — Sec 193 IPC** (now **§229 BNS, 2023**)
💡 Perjury = giving false evidence on oath. IPC §193 (renumbered as BNS §229) prescribes imprisonment up to 7 years + fine.
🏷️ Tag: `[NEET-PG 2020]`

**Q3.** A doctor in court refuses to answer a question that the judge has directed him to answer. He has committed:
A) Perjury
B) Contempt of court
C) Professional misconduct
D) Breach of secrecy

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Contempt of court**
💡 Refusal to answer a question that the court has directed = contempt of court. Perjury is giving false evidence on oath; misconduct relates to professional conduct outside court.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q4.** A doctor may refer to his case notes while deposing:
A) Without any permission
B) Only with the court's permission
C) Only if the opposing lawyer permits
D) Never

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Only with the court's permission**
💡 The doctor should carry his records but may consult them while deposing only after obtaining the court's permission. Refreshing memory from records is a recognised practice.
🏷️ Tag: `[UNIV]`

---

**Q5.** All of the following are correct conduct for a doctor giving evidence in court **except**:
A) Take oath before deposing
B) Volunteer additional information he thinks might be useful
C) Answer in plain language
D) Carry the postmortem report to court

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Volunteer additional information he thinks might be useful**
💡 Answer only what is asked. Volunteering information weakens credibility and exposes the doctor to traps in cross-examination.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q6.** A doctor in court is asked to disclose his patient's HIV status. He should:
A) Disclose immediately
B) Refuse — patient confidentiality is absolute
C) Disclose only if the court directs him after considering the request
D) Inform the patient first and then refuse

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: C — Disclose only if the court directs him after considering the request**
💡 Professional secrecy is not absolute. If the court, after weighing the request, directs disclosure, the doctor must comply. He cannot refuse a lawful court order.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`
