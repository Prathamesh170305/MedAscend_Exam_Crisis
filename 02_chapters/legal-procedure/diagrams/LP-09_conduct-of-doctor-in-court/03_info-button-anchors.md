# LP-09 — ⓘ-Button Anchors

---

### NODE: DO — Be punctual / dress formally / address the court properly
- **Start sentence:** "A doctor summoned to give evidence should reach the court well before the scheduled time."
- **End sentence:** "He should address the presiding officer appropriately — 'My Lord' or 'Your Honour' in a High Court / Supreme Court, and 'Sir' in subordinate courts."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Conduct of Doctor in Court; Source B p.11 -->

### NODE: DO — Carry records / refer with permission
- **Start sentence:** "The doctor should bring with him all relevant records — medicolegal report, postmortem report, and case notes — to the court."
- **End sentence:** "He may refer to them while deposing only with the prior permission of the court."
<!-- Backend source: Source A; Source B p.11 -->

### NODE: DO — Take oath before deposing
- **Start sentence:** "Before deposing, the doctor must take an oath administered by the court to speak the truth."
- **End sentence:** "Refusal to take oath without sufficient cause is itself an offence."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Procedure in Court; Source B p.11 -->

### NODE: DON'T — Don't lose temper / argue
- **Start sentence:** "During cross-examination, the doctor must remain calm and composed, regardless of the line of questioning."
- **End sentence:** "He should never enter into an argument with the lawyer or the judge."
<!-- Backend source: Source A; Source B p.11 -->

### NODE: DON'T — Don't volunteer information / speculate
- **Start sentence:** "The doctor should answer only the specific question asked and confine his replies to his own professional knowledge and opinion."
- **End sentence:** "He should not speculate beyond the evidence on record."
<!-- Backend source: Source A; Source B p.11 -->

### NODE: DON'T — Don't disclose patient secrets / take sides
- **Start sentence:** "The doctor is bound by professional secrecy and must not disclose information about his patient without the patient's consent or an explicit direction of the court."
- **End sentence:** "He should remain neutral and not take sides in the dispute."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Professional Secrecy; Source B Ch 2 (Medical Jurisprudence — Consent / Secrecy) p.20-23 -->

### NODE: DON'T — Don't refuse a court-directed question
- **Start sentence:** "If the court directs the witness to answer a question, refusal without lawful excuse amounts to contempt of court."
- **End sentence:** "The doctor's only legitimate ground for declining is the protection of privileged communication recognised by law."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Contempt of Court; Source B p.11 -->

### NODE: DON'T — Perjury / false evidence
- **Start sentence:** "Giving false evidence on oath in court is the offence of perjury."
- **End sentence:** "Perjury is punishable under section 193 of the Indian Penal Code (now section 229 of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023) with imprisonment that may extend to seven years and fine."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Perjury; Source B p.11; current law: IPC §193 → BNS §229 -->
