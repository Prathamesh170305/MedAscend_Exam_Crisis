# LP-04 — ⓘ-Button Anchors

---

### NODE: MEDICAL EVIDENCE (root)
- **Start sentence:** "Medical evidence is the evidence given by a medical practitioner in his professional capacity."
- **End sentence:** "It may be classified as documentary, oral, real, hearsay, or circumstantial."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Medical Evidence; Source B p.7 -->

### NODE: DOCUMENTARY evidence
- **Start sentence:** "Documentary evidence consists of evidence in the form of documents produced before the court."
- **End sentence:** "Examples include medical certificates, medicolegal reports, postmortem reports, dying declarations, and affidavits."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Documentary Evidence; Source B p.7 -->

### NODE: ORAL evidence
- **Start sentence:** "Oral evidence is the statement made by a witness in the court under oath."
- **End sentence:** "It also includes signs and gestures of a witness who cannot speak, and the written statement of a mute witness."
<!-- Backend source: Source A; Source B p.8 -->

### NODE: REAL / MATERIAL evidence
- **Start sentence:** "Real or material evidence consists of physical objects produced before the court for examination."
- **End sentence:** "Examples include weapons, clothing, hair, bullets, viscera, and bodily stains."
<!-- Backend source: Source A; Source B p.8 -->

### NODE: HEARSAY evidence
- **Start sentence:** "Hearsay evidence is what a witness has heard from another person about the matter in dispute."
- **End sentence:** "It is generally not admissible, but exceptions include a dying declaration, statements forming part of res gestae, and entries in public records."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Hearsay; Source B p.8; cross-refs to BSA 2023 §26 (dying declaration) -->

### NODE: CIRCUMSTANTIAL evidence
- **Start sentence:** "Circumstantial evidence is indirect evidence from which the existence of a fact in dispute is inferred."
- **End sentence:** "An example is estimating the time since death from postmortem changes such as rigor mortis or lividity."
<!-- Backend source: Source A; Source B p.8 -->
