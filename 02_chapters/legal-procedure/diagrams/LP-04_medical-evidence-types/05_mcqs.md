# LP-04 — MCQs (Types of Medical Evidence)

---

**Q1.** Which of the following is **generally inadmissible** as evidence, with specific exceptions?
A) Documentary
B) Oral
C) Real
D) Hearsay

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: D — Hearsay**
💡 Hearsay (what a witness heard from another person) is generally inadmissible. Recognised exceptions: dying declaration, res gestae, entries in public records.

**Q2.** A dying declaration is admissible as evidence under which section of the 2023 evidence code?
A) §26 BSA (Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam)
B) §39 BSA
C) §45 IEA
D) §32 BSA

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: A — §26 BSA**
💡 BSA 2023 §26 (corresponding to old IEA §32) makes the dying declaration an admissible exception to the hearsay rule. Expert evidence sits under §39 BSA (old §45 IEA).

**Q3.** A postmortem report submitted to a court falls under which category of evidence?
A) Oral
B) Documentary
C) Real
D) Circumstantial

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Documentary**
💡 The PM report is a document — therefore documentary evidence. The viscera or weapon attached separately would be real evidence; the doctor's spoken testimony would be oral.

**Q4.** A weapon retrieved from a crime scene and produced in court is an example of:
A) Real evidence
B) Documentary evidence
C) Hearsay
D) Circumstantial

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: A — Real evidence**
💡 Real (material) evidence = physical object produced before the court. Includes weapons, clothing, viscera, bullets, hair, stains.
🏷️ Tag: `[UNIV]`

---

**Q5.** Determination of time since death from the degree of rigor mortis is an example of:
A) Documentary evidence
B) Oral evidence
C) Circumstantial evidence
D) Real evidence

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: C — Circumstantial evidence**
💡 Circumstantial evidence is indirect — a fact (time of death) is inferred from another fact (degree of rigor). Different from real evidence, which is the physical object itself.

---

**Q6.** "Res gestae" exception to hearsay refers to statements that are:
A) Made under oath in court
B) Made in advance to police
C) Made spontaneously at or about the time of the event in question
D) Made by an expert witness only

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: C — Made spontaneously at or about the time of the event**
💡 Res gestae = statements forming part of the same transaction, made spontaneously, so close in time that fabrication is unlikely. Admissible despite being technically hearsay.
