# LP-07 — ⓘ-Button Anchors

---

### NODE: Step 1 — OATH
- **Start sentence:** "Before the witness deposes, he is required to take an oath administered by the court that he will speak the truth, the whole truth, and nothing but the truth."
- **End sentence:** "Refusal to take oath without sufficient cause is punishable."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Procedure in Court; Source B p.10 -->

### NODE: Step 2 — EXAMINATION-IN-CHIEF
- **Start sentence:** "Examination-in-chief is the examination of a witness by the party who called him."
- **End sentence:** "Leading questions are not allowed, except with the permission of the court."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Examination-in-chief; Source B p.10 -->

### NODE: Step 3 — CROSS-EXAMINATION
- **Start sentence:** "Cross-examination is the examination of a witness by the adverse party."
- **End sentence:** "Leading questions are allowed in cross-examination, and its primary purpose is to test the credibility of the witness."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Cross-examination; Source B p.10 -->

### NODE: Step 4 — RE-EXAMINATION
- **Start sentence:** "Re-examination is the examination of a witness, subsequent to cross-examination, by the party that called him."
- **End sentence:** "It is restricted to clarification of matters raised in cross-examination; new matter can be introduced only with the permission of the court."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Re-examination; Source B p.10 -->

### NODE: Step 5 — COURT QUESTIONS
- **Start sentence:** "The judge may put any question to the witness at any stage of the deposition for clarification."
- **End sentence:** "Such questions are not subject to objection by either party, as they are intended to assist the court in arriving at a just decision."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Court Questions; Source B p.11 -->

### NODE: Leading question (concept)
- **Start sentence:** "A leading question is one which suggests the answer that the questioner wishes to receive."
- **End sentence:** "Leading questions are permitted in cross-examination, but generally not in examination-in-chief or re-examination."
<!-- Backend source: Source A — Leading Question; Source B p.10 — cross-ref with LP-08 -->
