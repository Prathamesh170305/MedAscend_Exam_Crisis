# LP-07 — MCQs (Witness Examination Procedure)

---

**Q1.** While recording evidence in the court of law, lawyer asked the witness, "Were you present when A killed B?", and the witness answered "yes". This type of questioning is permitted in?
A) Examination in chief
B) Direct examination
C) Redirect examination
D) Cross examination

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: D — Cross examination**
💡 The question "Were you present when A killed B?" suggests its own answer — it is a **leading question**. Leading questions are permitted **only in cross-examination** (and from the judge); not in examination-in-chief, direct, or re-examination.

**Q2.** During the court proceedings, the defense lawyer asks a leading question, which the prosecutor appeals against. The judge grants the appeal. Leading questions are not allowed in all of the following except?
A) Re-examination
B) Cross-examination
C) Examination in chief
D) Dying declaration

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Cross-examination**
💡 Leading questions are **not allowed** in examination-in-chief and re-examination. They **are allowed** in cross-examination — that's the exception.
🏷️ Tag: `[NEET-PG 2022]`

**Q3.** The correct sequence of phases of witness examination in court is:
A) Oath → Cross-examination → Examination-in-chief → Re-examination
B) Oath → Examination-in-chief → Cross-examination → Re-examination
C) Examination-in-chief → Oath → Cross-examination → Re-examination
D) Examination-in-chief → Re-examination → Cross-examination → Oath

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Oath → Examination-in-chief → Cross-examination → Re-examination**
💡 Oath comes first; the calling party then conducts examination-in-chief; the opposing party then cross-examines; finally the calling party re-examines (clarification only). The judge may interject with court questions at any stage.

---

**Q4.** Re-examination is permitted to:
A) Introduce entirely new evidence
B) Clarify points raised in cross-examination
C) Discredit the cross-examiner
D) Replace the oath

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Clarify points raised in cross-examination**
💡 Re-examination is restricted to clarifying matters arising in cross-examination. Introduction of new matter requires the court's leave.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q5.** A judge can put questions to a witness:
A) Only at the start of the deposition
B) Only after re-examination is over
C) At any stage of the deposition
D) Only when both parties agree

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: C — At any stage of the deposition**
💡 The judge may interject with clarifying questions at any time; such questions are not subject to objection.
🏷️ Tag: `[UNIV]`

---

**Q6.** The primary purpose of cross-examination is to:
A) Establish facts of the case
B) Test credibility of the witness
C) Permit re-examination
D) Replace direct examination

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Test credibility of the witness**
💡 Examination-in-chief = elicit favourable facts. Cross-examination = test credibility / expose inconsistencies. Re-examination = clarify cross. Different purposes for different phases.
