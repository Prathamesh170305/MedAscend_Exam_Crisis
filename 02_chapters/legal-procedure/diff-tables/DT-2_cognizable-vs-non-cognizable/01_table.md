**DT-2 — Cognizable vs Non-cognizable Offence**
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

| Feature | Cognizable Offence | Non-cognizable Offence |
|---------|---------------------|--------------------------|
| **Definition** | Serious offence where police may arrest **without warrant** | Less serious offence; police **need a warrant** to arrest |
| **Police powers** | Investigate without magistrate's order | Cannot investigate without magistrate's permission |
| **Typical examples** | Murder, rape, grievous hurt, dacoity, kidnapping, dowry death | Simple hurt, defamation, public nuisance, criminal trespass, adultery (when criminalised) |
| **Severity / Punishment** | Generally ≥ 3 years imprisonment | Generally < 3 years |
| **FIR registration** | Mandatory — police MUST register an FIR | Optional; police record in a "Non-Cognizable Report" |
| **Bail** | Often **non-bailable** (depends on specific offence) | Usually **bailable** |
| **Inquest applicability** | All sudden / suspicious / unnatural deaths fall here | Not relevant — inquest is about death investigation |
| **Trial court** | Sessions / Magistrate (depending on offence) | Magistrate Court |
| **Current-law tag** | BNS / BNSS 2023 (replaces IPC / CrPC) | BNS / BNSS 2023 |
