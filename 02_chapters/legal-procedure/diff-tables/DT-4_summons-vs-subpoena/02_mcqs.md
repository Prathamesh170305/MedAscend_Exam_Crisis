# DT-4 — MCQs (Summons vs Subpoena)

---

**Q1.** "Subpoena duces tecum" refers to:
A) Witness summons
B) Production summons
C) Arrest warrant
D) Search warrant

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Production summons**
💡 "Subpoena duces tecum" = bring documents with you. "Subpoena ad testificandum" = come and testify.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q2.** The Latin meaning of "subpoena" is:
A) "By the court"
B) "Under penalty"
C) "To be present"
D) "On the record"

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Under penalty**
💡 "Sub poena" literally = under penalty — emphasising that disobedience is punishable.
🏷️ Tag: `[UNIV]`

---

**Q3.** In Indian medicolegal practice, a doctor receiving a "subpoena" should treat it as:
A) An advisory note
B) Equivalent to a summons
C) A police warrant
D) An invitation

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Equivalent to a summons**
💡 Both compel attendance / production; disobedience invites contempt. Functionally identical.
