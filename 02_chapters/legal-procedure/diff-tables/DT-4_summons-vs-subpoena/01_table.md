**DT-4 — Summons vs Subpoena**
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

> Functionally **the same document**, named differently in different jurisdictions. The exam value lies in knowing the equivalence — and the etymology / sub-types.

| Feature | Summons (Indian usage) | Subpoena (foreign / common-law usage) |
|---------|--------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| **Etymology** | Latin "to call" | Latin "sub poena" = **"under penalty"** |
| **Jurisdiction** | Indian courts — the standard term | Used in UK, USA, and other common-law countries |
| **Function** | Written court order to attend / produce | Same — written court order to attend / produce |
| **Types** | Witness summons + Production summons | "Subpoena ad testificandum" (witness) + "Subpoena duces tecum" (production) |
| **Issued by** | Court — signed by judge, bears court seal | Court — same formalities |
| **Service** | In duplicate; original served, duplicate signed and returned | Same |
| **Disobedience** | Punishable as contempt of court | Punishable; literal meaning "under penalty" emphasises this |
| **Conduct money** | Paid in civil cases | Equivalent provision exists |
| **Current Indian law** | BNSS 2023 (replaces CrPC provisions) | — |
