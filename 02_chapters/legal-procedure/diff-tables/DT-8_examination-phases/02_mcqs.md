# DT-8 — MCQs (Examination Phases)

---

**Q1.** Leading questions are permitted in:
A) Examination-in-chief
B) Cross-examination only
C) Re-examination
D) All three phases

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Cross-examination only**
💡 Leading questions are allowed only in cross-examination (and the judge's questions). NOT allowed in examination-in-chief or re-examination.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q2.** Re-examination is conducted by:
A) The opposing party, to discredit the witness
B) The calling party, to clarify points raised in cross-examination
C) The judge directly
D) The calling party, to introduce new evidence

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — The calling party, to clarify points raised in cross-examination**
💡 Re-exam = calling party again, restricted to clarifying cross-exam material. New matter requires the court's permission.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q3.** The primary purpose of cross-examination is to:
A) Confirm the witness's case
B) Test the credibility of the witness
C) Take a sworn statement
D) Establish the doctor's qualifications

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Test the credibility of the witness**
💡 Cross-exam = credibility-test phase. The opposing party probes for inconsistencies, bias, exaggeration.
🏷️ Tag: `[UNIV]`

---

**Q4.** Which of the following is the correct sequence?
A) Cross → Chief → Re-exam
B) Chief → Cross → Re-exam
C) Chief → Re-exam → Cross
D) Re-exam → Chief → Cross

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Chief → Cross → Re-exam**
💡 Oath first, then Examination-in-chief, then Cross-examination, then Re-examination. Court questions can come at any stage.
🏷️ Tag: `[UNIV]`

---

**Q5.** In re-examination, introducing new matter not raised in cross-examination requires:
A) Permission of the opposing party
B) Permission of the court
C) Permission of the witness
D) No permission — it is free

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Permission of the court**
💡 Re-exam is normally confined to cross-exam material; new matter requires leave of the court.
