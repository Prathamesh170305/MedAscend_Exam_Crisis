**DT-8 — Examination-in-Chief vs Cross-Examination vs Re-Examination**
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

| Feature | Examination-in-Chief | Cross-Examination | Re-Examination |
|---------|------------------------|---------------------|--------------------|
| **Conducted by** | The party that **called** the witness | The **opposing** party | The party that **called** the witness (again) |
| **Sequence** | 1st (after oath) | 2nd | 3rd |
| **Purpose** | Elicit facts that **support the calling party's case** | **Test credibility** — expose inconsistencies, elicit favourable facts | **Clarify matters raised in cross**; rehabilitate the witness |
| **Leading questions** | ❌ **NOT allowed** (except with court's permission) | ✅ **ALLOWED** | ❌ **NOT allowed** |
| **Scope** | Wide — any relevant fact within the witness's knowledge | Wide — extends beyond facts mentioned in chief, including credit-testing | **Narrow** — confined to matters raised in cross; new matter only with court's leave |
| **Evidentiary value** | Establishes the case-in-chief | **Most important phase** — credibility of the witness stands or falls here | Damage-control / clarification |
| **Doctor's tip** | Stick to your written report; answer plainly | Stay composed; do not lose temper; answer only what is asked | Use sparingly to clarify any misinterpretation in cross |
| **Court questions** | Judge may interject at any point | Same | Same |
