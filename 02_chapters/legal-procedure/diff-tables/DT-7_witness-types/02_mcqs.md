# DT-7 — MCQs (Common vs Expert vs Hostile Witness)

---

**Q1.** A common witness in court:
A) Can give opinion in his area of interest
B) Can only state facts perceived by him
C) Is the same as an expert witness
D) Cannot be cross-examined

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Can only state facts perceived by him**
💡 Common witness = facts only, no opinion. Opinion privilege belongs to expert witnesses.
🏷️ Tag: `[UNIV]`

---

**Q2.** A doctor appearing in court is considered which type of witness?
A) Common
B) Expert (skilled)
C) Hostile
D) Eyewitness

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Expert (skilled)**
💡 A doctor is permitted to give opinion under BSA §39 (old IEA §45). Common witnesses cannot opine.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q3.** A witness can be cross-examined by the party that called him only after:
A) Re-examination
B) The witness has been declared "hostile" by the court
C) Permission from the police
D) The opposing party agrees

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — The witness has been declared "hostile" by the court**
💡 The calling party normally cannot cross-examine its own witness. Only after the court formally declares him hostile is this permitted.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q4.** Expert opinions are admitted as relevant facts under which section of the 2023 evidence code?
A) BSA §26
B) BSA §39
C) BSA §65
D) BSA §17

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — BSA §39**
💡 BSA 2023 §39 (renumbered from IEA §45) governs expert opinion evidence. BSA §26 (from IEA §32) is the dying-declaration exception.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`
