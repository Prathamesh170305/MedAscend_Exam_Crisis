**DT-7 — Common Witness vs Expert Witness vs Hostile Witness**
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

| Feature | Common Witness | Expert Witness | Hostile Witness |
|---------|------------------|------------------|--------------------|
| **Synonym** | Witness of fact | Skilled witness | — |
| **Definition** | Deposes about facts seen / heard / perceived by himself | Specially skilled in a subject (science, art, foreign law, handwriting) | Witness who, after being called, gives evidence against the case of the calling party |
| **Can give opinion?** | ❌ **NO** — facts only | ✅ **YES** — opinion within his expertise | Either — depends on prior status |
| **Legal basis for opinion** | — | **BSA §39** (old IEA §45) | — |
| **Oath required?** | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |
| **Subject to cross-examination?** | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes — and the calling party may cross-examine its OWN witness with court's permission |
| **Examples** | Eyewitness to accident, neighbour who heard the cry | Forensic doctor, ballistics expert, handwriting analyst, psychiatrist | A complainant who, mid-trial, denies the police's version |
| **Role of judge** | Receives oral testimony | Receives opinion evidence — may give it weight as the court sees fit | **Must formally declare** the witness "hostile" before cross-examination by own side |
| **In medicolegal practice** | Bystanders, panchas | The doctor appearing in court is treated as an expert witness | Common in cases of witness intimidation or compromise |
