# DT-3 — MCQs (Bailable vs Non-bailable)

---

**Q1.** In a bailable offence:
A) Bail is a matter of right for the accused
B) Bail is at the discretion of the police only
C) Bail can never be granted
D) Bail is granted only after trial

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: A — Bail is a matter of right for the accused**
💡 Bailable = bail is a right; the police or court must grant on application. Non-bailable = bail is discretionary, granted only by the court.

---

**Q2.** Which of the following is a non-bailable offence?
A) Defamation
B) Murder
C) Simple hurt
D) Public nuisance

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Murder**
💡 Murder (BNS §103) is heinous → non-bailable, cognizable, tried in Sessions Court. The other three are typically bailable.
🏷️ Tag: `[UNIV]`

---

**Q3.** Bail in a non-bailable offence:
A) Cannot be granted under any circumstances
B) Can be granted only by the court, at its discretion
C) Is granted only by police
D) Is automatic after 7 days in custody

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Can be granted only by the court, at its discretion**
💡 Police cannot grant bail in non-bailable offences. Only a court can — and even then, it's discretionary, not a right.
