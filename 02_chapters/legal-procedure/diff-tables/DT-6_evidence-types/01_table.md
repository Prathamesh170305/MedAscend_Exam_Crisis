**DT-6 — Documentary vs Oral vs Hearsay Evidence**
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

| Feature | Documentary | Oral | Hearsay |
|---------|--------------|--------|----------|
| **Definition** | Evidence in the form of documents produced before the court | Statement of a witness in court under oath | What a witness heard from another person about the matter |
| **Examples** | Medical certificate, MLR, postmortem report, **dying declaration**, affidavit, X-rays | Doctor's oral deposition in court; signs/gestures of a mute witness | A friend's report of what the victim said before death |
| **Admissibility** | ✅ Admissible | ✅ Admissible — generally the **best evidence** | ❌ Generally **NOT admissible** |
| **Recorded form** | Written / recorded permanently | Spoken; transcribed by court clerk | Reported through another's mouth |
| **Source proximity to fact** | Direct (the document IS the record) | Direct (witness perceived the fact) | **Indirect** (second-hand) |
| **Cross-examination needed** | Yes — author may be cross-examined on the document | Yes — witness is cross-examined | Often impossible — the original speaker is absent |
| **Exceptions / special status** | Dying declaration is documentary AND survives as an exception to hearsay | — | Exceptions: **dying declaration, res gestae, public records, expert opinion in textbooks** |
| **Current-law section (2023)** | BSA — relevant chapters | BSA | BSA §26 (dying declaration exception) |
