# DT-6 — MCQs (Documentary vs Oral vs Hearsay Evidence)

---

**Q1.** Hearsay evidence is generally inadmissible, but the following is an admitted exception:
A) Affidavit
B) Dying declaration
C) Police investigation report
D) Medical certificate

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Dying declaration**
💡 Dying declaration is technically hearsay but admissible under BSA §26 (old IEA §32). Other exceptions: res gestae, public records, expert opinion in published textbooks.

---

**Q2.** A doctor's deposition in court is which type of evidence?
A) Documentary
B) Oral
C) Hearsay
D) Real

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Oral**
💡 The doctor's spoken testimony = oral evidence. The PM report he produced separately = documentary evidence. Different categories despite being from the same doctor.

---

**Q3.** The "best evidence" rule generally favours:
A) Documentary evidence over oral
B) Oral evidence — direct perception, subject to cross-exam
C) Hearsay — readily available
D) Circumstantial — most inferential

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Oral evidence — direct perception, subject to cross-exam**
💡 Oral evidence by a witness who directly perceived the fact and can be cross-examined is the gold standard. Documentary requires the author to also testify to its authenticity.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q4.** "Res gestae" admission exception applies to:
A) Statements forming part of the same transaction, made spontaneously
B) Statements made after deliberation
C) Statements made by experts only
D) Statements admitting guilt

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: A — Statements forming part of the same transaction, made spontaneously**
💡 Res gestae = spontaneous statements so close in time to the event that fabrication is unlikely. Admissible despite being hearsay.
