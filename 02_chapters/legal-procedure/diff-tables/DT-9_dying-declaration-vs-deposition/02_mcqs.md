# DT-9 — MCQs (Dying Declaration vs Dying Deposition)

---

**Q1.** Which of the following is the **defining difference** between a dying declaration and a dying deposition?
A) Patient's condition
B) Oath + cross-examination
C) Recording paper used
D) Doctor's involvement

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Oath + cross-examination**
💡 Dying declaration → **no oath, no cross-exam**. Dying deposition → **oath + cross-exam**, with accused/pleader present. This is the high-yield distinction.

---

**Q2.** A dying declaration is admissible:
A) Even if the declarant survives
B) Only if the declarant dies
C) Only if recorded by a magistrate
D) Only in cases of poisoning

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Only if the declarant dies**
💡 Admissibility under BSA §26 (old IEA §32) is contingent on the declarant's death. If he survives, his statement is treated as an ordinary statement.

---

**Q3.** Which has higher evidentiary value?
A) Dying declaration
B) Dying deposition
C) Both equal
D) Depends on the court

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Dying deposition**
💡 Dying deposition has higher value — it includes oath + cross-examination, making it equivalent to a court deposition.

---

**Q4.** A 56-year-old woman, victim of domestic violence, is admitted to hospital and gives a statement about who assaulted her. The most authoritative recorder of this statement is:
A) The treating doctor
B) The police officer
C) **A Magistrate**
D) Hospital administrator

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: C — A Magistrate**
💡 Ideal recorder = Magistrate (Executive or Judicial). Police / doctor can also record if a magistrate is not available, but a magistrate-recorded statement carries the highest weight. Falls under BSA §26.

---

**Q5.** Oath is **NOT** administered for a dying declaration because:
A) The dying person is presumed to speak the truth
B) Oath is administered after recording
C) Indian law does not require oath
D) The patient is unconscious

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: A — The dying person is presumed to speak the truth**
💡 The Latin maxim "nemo moriturus praesumitur mentiri" — no one about to die is presumed to lie — is the legal foundation. This makes the un-sworn statement admissible.
