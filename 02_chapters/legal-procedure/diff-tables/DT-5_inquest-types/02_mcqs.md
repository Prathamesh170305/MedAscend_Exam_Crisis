# DT-5 — MCQs (Inquest Types)

---

**Q1.** Police inquest is conducted under:
A) §174 CrPC / §194 BNSS
B) §176 CrPC / §196 BNSS
C) §172 CrPC
D) Coroner's Act

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: A — §174 CrPC / §194 BNSS**
💡 §174 CrPC (now §194 BNSS 2023) governs police inquest. §176 (→ §196 BNSS) governs Magistrate inquest.

---

**Q2.** A custodial death must be subjected to:
A) Police inquest
B) Magistrate inquest
C) Coroner inquest
D) Medical Examiner system

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Magistrate inquest**
💡 Magistrate inquest is mandatory in: custodial death, dowry death (< 7 yrs), exhumation, death in psychiatric hospital. The higher evidentiary value is the rationale.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

**Q3.** Coroner's inquest was abolished in Mumbai in the year:
A) 1989
B) 1995
C) 1999
D) 2005

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: C — 1999** (29 July 1999)
💡 The last Coroner's court in India (Mumbai) was abolished on 29 July 1999. Kolkata had abolished it earlier.
🏷️ Tag: `[UNIV]`

---

**Q4.** Panchnama is the report prepared in:
A) Police inquest
B) Magistrate inquest
C) Coroner inquest
D) Medical Examiner inquest

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: A — Police inquest**
💡 Panchnama = IO's report at the scene, signed by 2+ local witnesses (Panchas). Specific to police inquest.
🏷️ Tag: `[UNIV]`

---

**Q5.** "Open verdict" by a Coroner means:
A) The accused is openly identified
B) Crime is confirmed but the accused is unknown
C) The inquiry is open to the public
D) No crime occurred

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Crime is confirmed but the accused is unknown**
💡 Open verdict = announcement of commission of crime without information regarding the accused.
