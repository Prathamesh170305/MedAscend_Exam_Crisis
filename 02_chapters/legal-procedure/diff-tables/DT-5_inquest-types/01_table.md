**DT-5 — Police Inquest vs Magistrate Inquest vs Coroner Inquest**
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

| Feature | Police Inquest | Magistrate Inquest | Coroner Inquest |
|---------|------------------|----------------------|--------------------|
| **Conducted by** | Investigating Officer (≥ Sub-Inspector rank) | Executive Magistrate | Coroner (rank of First Class Magistrate; could be doctor or lawyer) |
| **Legal basis** | **§174 CrPC** (now **§194 BNSS, 2023**) | **§176 CrPC** (now **§196 BNSS, 2023**) | Coroner's Act |
| **When mandatory** | Sudden / suspicious / unnatural deaths (RTAs, burns, falls, found bodies) | Custodial death, dowry death (within 7 yrs of marriage), exhumation, death in psychiatric hospital | Was held only in cases of unnatural / suspicious deaths in jurisdiction |
| **Report / verdict** | **Panchnama** (signed by IO + Panchas) | Magistrate's inquest report | **Verdict** — finding on cause of death; "open verdict" if accused unknown |
| **Witnesses required** | 2+ Panchas (responsible local persons) | Magistrate's own staff | Coroner's court — examines witnesses on oath |
| **Evidentiary weight** | Lower (administrative) | **Higher** (judicial) | Court of inquiry only — cannot try the case |
| **Current status in India** | ✅ Practised — most common | ✅ Practised | ❌ Abolished — **Mumbai 29 July 1999**; Kolkata earlier |
| **Equivalent abroad** | — | — | Still practised in UK |
| **Best suited for** | Routine sudden/unnatural deaths | Politically / legally sensitive deaths | (historical) — replaced by Medical Examiner system in USA |
