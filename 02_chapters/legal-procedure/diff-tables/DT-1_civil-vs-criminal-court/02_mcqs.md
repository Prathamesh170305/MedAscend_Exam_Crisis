# DT-1 — MCQs (Civil vs Criminal Court)

---

**Q1.** Burden of proof in a criminal trial is:
A) Preponderance of probability
B) Beyond reasonable doubt
C) Balance of convenience
D) On both parties equally

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Beyond reasonable doubt**
💡 The higher standard ("beyond reasonable doubt") is the hallmark of criminal trials. Civil cases use the lower "preponderance of probability".

---

**Q2.** A medical-negligence damages claim filed by a patient against a doctor is tried in:
A) Sessions Court
B) Civil Court
C) Magistrate Court
D) Coroner's Court

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: B — Civil Court**
💡 Damages claims are private disputes between plaintiff (patient) and defendant (doctor) → Civil Court. If the negligence is gross / rash, a separate criminal case under §304-A IPC / §106 BNS may also be filed in a Criminal Court.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q3.** Conduct money is paid to a witness in:
A) Civil cases
B) Criminal cases
C) Both
D) Neither

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: A — Civil cases**
💡 Civil cases → conduct money paid in advance. Criminal cases → expenses reimbursed by the government after witness attends court.
🏷️ Tag: `[PG-Inspired]`

---

**Q4.** Which of the following matters is heard in a criminal court?
A) Recovery of debt
B) Divorce
C) Grievous hurt
D) Property dispute

🔓 Reveal Answer
✅ **Correct: C — Grievous hurt**
💡 Grievous hurt is an offence against the state — criminal court. Debt, divorce, and property = civil disputes.
