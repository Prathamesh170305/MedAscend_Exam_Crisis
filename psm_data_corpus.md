## 4.3 Diseases Covered (12 total)

**Nationally (9):** Diphtheria · Pertussis · Tetanus · Polio · Measles · Rubella · Severe childhood TB · Hepatitis B · Hib (Meningitis & Pneumonia)
**Sub-nationally (3):** Rotavirus diarrhoea · Pneumococcal pneumonia · Japanese Encephalitis

## 4.4 Upper Age Limits (catch-up rules)

| Vaccine | Upper age limit |
|---------|-----------------|
| BCG | 1 year |
| OPV | 5 years |
| Measles/MR | 5 years (campaign mode: up to 15 years) |
| DPT | 7 years |
| JE | 15 years (endemic) |
| Pentavalent, IPV, PCV, RVV | 1 year (must start before 1 yr; else not given under UIP) |

## 4.5 Key Definitions

- **Fully immunised child:** Received all UIP vaccines within 1st year of life
- **Completely immunised:** Received 1st year + 2nd year schedule (incl. boosters)
- **Left-outs:** Never vaccinated
- **Drop-outs:** Started but not completed

## 4.6 Major Milestones

- **Polio eradication:** Declared in 2014
- **Maternal & neonatal tetanus elimination:** 2015
- **TT → Td replacement:** To address adult diphtheria waning immunity
- **U-WIN:** Universal Immunisation digital platform, nationally rolled out 2024
- **Mission Indradhanush (MI):** Launched Dec 2014; target 90% full immunisation
- **Intensified Mission Indradhanush (IMI):** Launched 2017
- **Adult BCG initiative:** Sept 2024 — additional BCG dose for 18+ in 40 NTEP districts

## 6.1 BMW Rules — Biomedical Waste Management

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Current rule | **BMW Management Rules, 2016** (gazetted 28 March 2016) |
| Amendments | 2018, 2019 |
| Replaces | BMW (Management & Handling) Rules 1998 |
| Statute parent | Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 |
| Regulator | MoEFCC; CPCB at central; SPCB at state |
| Coverage | All HCFs, vaccination camps, blood camps, AYUSH camps, surgical camps |
| Categories | **Reduced from 10 to 4 colour-coded categories** |
| Pre-treatment | Lab/blood samples must be pre-treated per WHO/NACO guidelines |
| Storage limit | Untreated human/animal anatomical, soiled, biotechnology waste: **48 hours** |
| Captive treatment | Allowed only if CBWTF not within 75 km |
| Daily BMW generation/bed | ~0.25 kg per bed |
| India daily total | ~700–750 tonnes |

### Colour Coding (BMW 2016)

| Colour | Container | Waste Type | Treatment |
|--------|-----------|------------|-----------|
| **Yellow** | Non-chlorinated plastic bags | Human/animal anatomical, soiled, expired drugs (incl. cytotoxic), chemical, chemical liquid, microbiology/biotechnology, discarded linen contaminated with blood/body fluid | Incineration ≥1,200°C; plasma pyrolysis; deep burial (only rural HCF without access) |
| **Red** | Non-chlorinated plastic bags/containers | Contaminated recyclable plastic (tubing, bottles, IV sets, catheters, urine bags, syringes w/o needles, gloves) | Autoclaving/microwaving/hydroclaving + shredding → recycling |
| **White (translucent)** | Puncture-proof, leak-proof, tamper-proof containers | Waste sharps incl. metals (needles, syringes with fixed needles, scalpels, blades) | Autoclaving/dry heat sterilisation + shredding/mutilation → encapsulation/concrete pit |
| **Blue** | Cardboard box with blue marking OR puncture-proof, tamper-proof container | Broken/discarded/contaminated glassware (incl. medicine vials, ampoules) · metallic body implants | Disinfection (1% sodium hypochlorite) + autoclaving/microwaving → recycling |

### 2018 Amendment — Key Changes

- Phasing out of chlorinated plastic bags, gloves, blood bags by 27 March 2019
- Bar-coded bag/container system
- Pre-treatment of lab waste mandatory
- Annual report to SPCB by 30 June

### Cytotoxic drugs

- Returned to manufacturer OR sent to CBWTF for incineration at >1,200°C
- Yellow bag
